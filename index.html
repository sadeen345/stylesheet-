Covid 19

COVID-19 (Coronavirus Disease 2019) is a viral disease. The virus that causes it is called severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2.It is more commonly known as SARS-CoV-2. It began spreading in late 2019 and became a pandemic in 2020.
Symptoms
The typical symptoms of COVID-19 often appear two to 14 days after exposure to the virus.

Symptoms can include:
* Dry cough
*Shortness of breath.
* Loss of taste or smell.
* Extreme fatigue or exhaustion.
* Digestive symptoms such as upset stomach, vomiting, or loose stools (diarrhea).
* Pain, such as headache, body aches, or muscle aches Fever or chills.
* Fluid-like symptoms, such as congestion, runny nose, or sore throat.


When to see a doctor


Contact your healthcare professional if you test positive for COVID-19. If you have symptoms of COVID-19 and need to be tested, or if you have been exposed to someone with COVID-19, your healthcare professional can help. People at high risk of severe illness may be given medications to prevent the spread of COVID-19. Or, your healthcare team may plan regular tests to monitor your health.


Get immediate emergency medical help for any of the following symptoms:

* Difficulty or trouble breathing.
*Change in skin, lips, or nail beds to pale, gray, or blue.
*New-onset confusion.
*Difficulty waking up or staying awake.
*Persistent chest pain or pressure.

Coronavirus spreads primarily through person-to-person contact and can be transmitted from an infected person who has no symptoms. When COVID-19 patients cough, sneeze, breathe, sing, or talk, their breath may be contaminated with COVID-19.

The respiratory-transmitted coronavirus can land directly on the face of a nearby person after a sneeze or cough, for example. Other people can inhale the droplets or particles exhaled by infected people if they are close together or in an area with poor airflow. A person may touch a surface with infected respiratory droplets and then touch their face with their hand that has the coronavirus on it.
